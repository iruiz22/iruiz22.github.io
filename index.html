About Me

Hello! My name is Isabella Ruiz and I am a junior at Stanford University, majoring in Human Biology and minoring in Art Practice. My dream is to become a medical illustrator, and this year (2020), I have had lots of opportunities to build up my portfolio. Here is a collection of my sketches, finished graphite/charcoal pieces, and finished acrylic pieces I have made during this year. Unfortunately, not everything is anatomical. I do have to practice the basics before I can devote myself completely to the human body and all of its secrets. I suppose still lives are my vegetables and they should be eaten before dessert! My journey is far from over and I am far from perfect! But, I love drawing and I love anatomy, so while my journey will be long, it will be very fun. The human body is so fascinating and I can't wait to draw or paint everything about it!

Trigger/Content warning: Some of my sketches (on the sketches page) are of nude models and feature depictions of breasts, buttocks, and male/female genitalia. However, everything I have drawn has been purely to capture the human form.
